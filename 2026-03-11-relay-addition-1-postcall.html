2-Tap Post-Call Capture
Relay's MVP is blind to cellular phone calls. They don't appear in your calendar. They generate no signal. But they're often the warmest conversations in your pipeline — the prospect who says "just call me" is the one who's interested. This addition closes that blindspot with two taps, in 5 seconds, before you've put your phone down.

What Relay could — and couldn't — see
The core MVP captures everything via email and calendar. But there's one gap it couldn't close without a mobile moment.

Relay with this addition
  • Video calls (Zoom, Meet, Teams) — ✓ detected via calendar

  • Inbound "just call me" calls — ✓ detected via call log
  • WhatsApp voice calls — ✓ detected via CallKit (iOS) / call log (Android)
  • Post-call drafts for phone calls — ✓ queued via 2-tap outcome

No audio. No recording. Just call metadata — who, when, how long — and a notification that fires before you've moved on.

1
Incoming call — Priya Sharma
A call comes in from Priya Sharma's mobile number. Relay recognises it instantly — it matched the number against Priya's contact record when the app was first connected. The call log event fires: contact identified, duration tracking begins.

What Relay does in the background: logs call start time, timestamps the call against any calendar event within ±15 minutes (none here — this was a surprise call), confirms match confidence: High.
2
Call in progress — 23 minutes
The call runs. Relay does nothing during the call — no recording, no audio access, no monitoring. It simply waits for the call-end OS event.

On Android: READ_CALL_LOG permission detects call end.
On iOS: CallKit fires a call-ended event with duration and contact ID. Same mechanism used by Truecaller — no App Store risk.

Tap the red button on the phone to end the call.
3
Notification fires — 5 seconds after call ends
The notification appears while the founder is still holding the phone. Relay has pre-filled everything from the contact match — no typing required. One tap to expand, one tap to select outcome.

Design principle: No app open. No navigation. The notification itself is the interaction surface.
4
Tap an outcome — 4 options, one tap
Each option maps directly to a signal update. No free text. No form. Just the tap.

GOOD CALL

SENT PROPOSAL

DEAD
Warmth → Cold. Closed Lost suggested.
5
Data written to Relay

6
Tomorrow morning — digest surfaces the draft
If the outcome was Good call or Needs follow-up, the auto-draft job runs using the pre-call email thread as context plus the call outcome as a hint. By the next morning, this appears in the digest exactly as a post-calendar-meeting draft would.

What just changed in Relay
What if the founder ignores the notification?
No forced input. Ever. Here's what gets written when the notification is dismissed without a tap.
type: call_detected (not call_inferred — no outcome provided)
date: 2026-03-11 14:11
duration_minutes: 23
contact_id: priya-sharma-acme
call_outcome: null
warmth_change: none — no signal written
draft_triggered: false
The call appears in the activity timeline as an event. No warmth signal is written. No false data. The founder's deliberate silence is respected — better than a wrong signal.
